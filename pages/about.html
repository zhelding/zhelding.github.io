About Me

Hello! My name is Zoe Helding. I am a recent graduate from the University of Oxford with a Bachelor's Degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics and am currently working as a Data Analyst at 270 Strategies, a grassroots consultancy.

Both professionally and intellectually, my interests lie at the intersection of data, tech, and politics. In my hometown of Chicago, I served as a Data and Analytics intern on Governor Quinn's reelection campaign in 2014, developing and maintaining field reporting procedures. In the following summer, I travelled to Amman to provide strategic and logistical assistance to the UNDP Syria Sub-regional Response Facility. Academically, my thesis, "Repeat Challenger Revisitied: A Quantitative Study of the Effect of Campaign Spending on U.S. House Election Results", won a prize for the best undergraduate thesis in politics.

At the moment, I am working both on and off the job to aggressively expand my technical capabilities in full stack web development, data processing, and statistical analysis. Presently, I have experience building web apps using PostgreSQL, Node.js, HTML, CSS, Heroku, and Git as well as familiarity with Linux systems, Python, Ruby, and R.
